Whats LEGO KidsFest Like?
We get that question a lot.  Past attendees tell us its not like anything theyve ever experienced.  The LEGO KidsFest is about doing, not just looking.  Imagine dozens of hands-on activities, most of them that are all about LEGO, and many in ways you cant do at home or anywhere else.  We also partner with local attractions and educational organizations to bring additional family fun.  Mom, the kids, and even grandpa will get in on the interactive action.  For more of a taste of the LEGO KidsFest, CHECK OUT OUR VIDEO.  


Tickets are on sale now for all 2012 tour stops. 
 


New Additions
Just like new LEGO sets that appear on store shelves, we are always adding new features and attractions at the LEGO KidsFest.  Each tour stop is a little different because we also invite other organizations to join us in the fun.  Check your city listings on our website for the latest on exhibitors, sponsors, activities, hotel packages and more, with new information always added so be sure to check back often.

Tour Sponsors
Were thrilled to be partnering with these and other family-friendly companies as we tour the country:  
Going Green with Honest Kids:  Build fantastic forest-inspired green creations in our monochromatic build area and get a tasting of the yummy and organic Honest Kids drink.
BISSELL Sweeper Scramble Game:  Check it out in our Activity Area and throughout the LEGO KidsFest.  This is the perfect tool for picking up those small LEGO bricks that always seem to be underfoot. 
The Reading Rest Stop, presented by DK Publishing:   Enjoy books like DUPLO Rescue, LEGO Kingdoms: Defend the Castle, The LEGO Ideas Book and The Star Wars Visual Dictionary in comfortable furniture provided by Guidecraft.  

Stuff To Do
Hone your LEGO skills at home with the LEGO Star Wars Coloring Sheet and the LEGO Word Search, courtesy of DK Publishing. Find these activity sheets and more in our Reading Rest Stop and for sale at the LEGO KidsFest Marketplace. 

Why We Go Where We Go
Many of our fans have asked about this year's tour stops, and why we aren't revisiting some of last year's venues. With so much of the country to cover and so many kids wanting to come to a LEGO KidsFest, we are making every effort to visit new cities. With only about a half dozen stops each year, we want to spread the LEGO love.

We've visited many great cities over the past two years and have met tens of thousands of LEGO fans. We hope to return to many of these locations in future years, so keep following us on Facebook to get the latest news on upcoming tour dates.
